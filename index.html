Financial Statement Analysis Project

For this academic project, I analyzed company financial statements and used financial ratios to evaluate business performance. The project required me to calculate and interpret ratios such as gross profit rate, profit margin, return on assets, asset turnover, and debt-to-assets. Instead of only finding the numbers, I had to explain what the results showed about the company’s financial health.

This project helped me practice analytical thinking, spreadsheet organization, financial interpretation, and professional communication. These are important skills for a career in finance because employers need people who can understand data and explain what it means in a clear and useful way.

  • Used financial data to evaluate company performance.

Critical Thinking Reflection

Reflection on My Financial Statement Analysis Project

I chose my financial statement analysis project because it connects closely to my interest in business and finance. The project gave me a chance to take real company information and use it to make a judgment about how the company was performing. I liked this project because it was not just about memorizing formulas. It required me to think about what the numbers actually meant and how those results could affect business decisions.

Through this project, I learned how important it is to support opinions with evidence. For example, when looking at ratios like profit margin or debt-to-assets, I had to explain whether the number showed strength, weakness, improvement, or risk. I also learned that one ratio by itself does not tell the whole story. A company might improve in one area but still have problems in another, so it is important to look at the full financial picture.

The skills I learned from this project can help my career because finance requires accuracy, critical thinking, and communication. I practiced using formulas, organizing financial information, comparing companies, and turning calculations into professional recommendations. These skills would be useful in careers connected to financial analysis, commercial real estate, investing, accounting, or business management.

If I could do this project again, I would spend more time making the visual presentation cleaner and more professional from the beginning. I would also compare more years of financial data instead of focusing mainly on a shorter time period. This would help me see whether the company’s performance was part of a long-term trend or just a temporary change. I would also include more outside research about the company’s industry so my recommendations would be supported by both the financial ratios and the business environment.
